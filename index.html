Questions

The Ones An Operator At Your Stage Asks

Short, direct answers. The longer ones we cover on the call.

Why would I run this with an outside operator instead of in-house?

Because the team you have is already running the Mentorship. The CPD curriculum is being delivered. The member dashboard is live. The WhatsApp-to-Tom layer is staffed. Adding a 90-day build for a homepage VSL, an email capture and nurture, a premium-tier launch, and a paid acquisition layer on top of an already-loaded ops team is how those projects either get postponed by a year or get launched at half the quality. Outside operator is faster, cleaner, and exits when production hits steady state. We hand the system to your team after the launch cycle, not before.

How much of my time does this need?

Roughly half a day to record the homepage VSL. One day to record the Funded Trader Cohort positioning content. Calendar holds for cohort delivery in Lever 02. The rest is build team work plus you reviewing the copy, creative, and pricing decisions before they ship. Most operators at your stage spend under 8 hours a month during the 90-day build, and under 2 hours a month after the system is in production.

Will the Funded Trader Cohort cannibalise the Mentorship?

No. The Cohort and the Mentorship solve different problems. The Mentorship is a structured environment for traders building toward funding, with a CPD-accredited curriculum and 1:1 WhatsApp access. The Cohort is strategic operating-room access for funded traders who already trade live capital and want to scale to a multi-account portfolio. Different deliverable, different audience, different buying motion. Most Cohort applicants will be Mentorship graduates moving up the ladder, not net-new buyers choosing Cohort over Mentorship. Where there's overlap, the Cohort is the explicit "post-funded" tier. It expands the ladder, it doesn't compete inside it.

How does this work alongside my current team?

The build team operates alongside your existing structure. Your content team keeps producing. Your member-success team keeps running the dashboard and the WhatsApp layer. Your CPD-accredited curriculum delivery stays where it is. We layer on top: VSL pre-frame, lead magnet and nurture, Cohort sales engine, paid layer, alumni community. The only meaningful interface point is Tom on camera for the VSL and the Cohort positioning content. Everything else, we run autonomously and report back.

What if my existing Mentorship already covers some of what the Cohort would?

There's overlap on some content (risk management, execution discipline, market structure), but the deliverable is different. The Mentorship is "we structure the environment that gets you funded." The Cohort is "you already trade funded capital, here's how to operate at multi-account portfolio scale, with peer funded operators, in a strategic format that the Mentorship can't deliver." Most Cohort attendees will have already passed through the Mentorship and outgrown the WhatsApp-to-Tom format. The overlap is feature, not bug. Cohort buys insider continuity for funded operators who don't want to start from scratch with another mentor.

What if the Cohort doesn't fill?

The Cohort is sized at 6-8 seats first, sold to existing Mentorship alumni who've cleared funding (the warmest list for this offer that exists). If the first intake under-fills (say, 4 confirmed instead of 6), we don't push it into a half-empty room. We pause, sharpen the positioning and the application screen, push the date by 30-45 days, and try again with the second wave of alumni outreach plus any external warm sources we can verify. Build doesn't continue billing while the Cohort isn't running. If after a second attempt the audience isn't there at the modelled price, we restructure the tier or move the budget into Lever 03 paid acquisition.
